MSc Computing Project

The final 60-credit, 30-week dissertation project capping the MSc Artificial Intelligence. Topic, supervisor, and research question to be confirmed during Research Methods and Professional Practice (the preceding module).

The project will produce both a substantive written submission and a working artefact. As a working senior software engineer in healthcare, the topic is likely to sit at the intersection of applied AI in regulated, high-stakes domains — but this is intentionally open until the research-methods module shapes it.

Indicative research direction

Three candidate research areas being held in mind throughout the taught modules, each grounded in the questions raised on the About me page:

Candidate areaWhy it fitsModules that should sharpen it
Bias and accountability in AI-augmented clinical decision support Builds directly on Western Cape Department of Health work; Obermeyer et al. (2019) bias literature engaged in the Unit 1 forum task; clear practical and ethical stakes. Understanding AI; Machine Learning; Research Methods.
MLOps and governance patterns for regulated public-sector workloads Combines Azure / .NET / IaC engineering expertise with academic framing of governance, compliance, and operational resilience. Machine Learning; Intelligent Agents; Research Methods.
Architectural patterns for AI-augmented enterprise IT in healthcare Sits at the intersection of solution architecture (Azure SAA Expert), AI engineering (Azure AI Engineer), and the EITBOK / SWEBOK frameworks introduced in Launch into Computing. Numerical Analysis; Machine Learning; Research Methods.

Final selection deferred until Research Methods and Professional Practice; this list will be pruned to one focused question at that point.
