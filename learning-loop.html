Learning Loop

Programme-level synthesis tying learning across all modules.
UoEO e-Portfolio brief, §4.

What this is

Programme-level synthesis tying learning across every taught module of the MSc into one coherent trajectory. The UoEO e-Portfolio brief (§4) describes this as the artefact that gives a holistic perspective of the programme… should also include a review of Skills Matrices and action plans from individual modules and is ultimately shared with the Head of Computing as final feedback on the course.

Unlike the per-module reflective pieces — which look inward at a single 12-week block — the loop looks across modules for patterns: themes that recur, skills that compound, actions that carried over, ideas that changed how I work in practice.

Cross-module themes

Add one short paragraph per module on completion. Each paragraph names a theme — a question, a tension, a recurring idea — and connects it to at least one other module or to professional practice. To be populated as modules complete.

Consolidated skills matrix

Roll-up of the per-module skills matrices showing progression across the programme rather than a snapshot at one point in time. Skill list stable; only levels and evidence change. Populated cumulatively; first row appears after Launch into Computing completes.

Action-plan progression

Tracks which actions from each module's plan were completed, carried over, or abandoned with reason. Carried-over actions surviving two modules without progress get re-examined — usually either not actually important, or blocked on a dependency the plan didn't acknowledge.

Final handover note

Locked until end of programme. Will be a one-page reflection on the MSc as a whole — what worked, what didn't, what I'd change in the curriculum, and how the programme reshaped the questions I came in with. Shared with the Head of Computing as part of final course feedback.
